How this works

Two stages, your judgment in the middle

Every tool drafts options first. You compose: pick, reject, choose. Then it writes the full artifact from your selections. The result is labeled a draft because it is one; it enters your classroom only after your rework.

Where things run

This page is a static site. When you generate, your inputs travel to a private backend that holds the AI key and calls the model (currently Gemini, named here and nowhere else; the model behind the suite can change without the suite changing). Responses are checked against a strict structure before you ever see them; a malformed draft is rejected, not displayed.

Privacy

No accounts. Your inputs are processed to generate your draft and are not stored on the server. Usage logging is event-level only: which tool ran, how long it took, whether it worked. Never the content. Your drafts and inputs persist in your own browser so a refresh does not eat your work; Start over clears them.

Ask about a draft

The Ask panel answers only from the draft on your screen. If the answer is not in the material, it says so. It is permitted to disagree with your premise. It never writes anything addressed to students.
